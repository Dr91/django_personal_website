The picture in the homepage is the surface science instrument I have designed and built during my Ph.D study in chemistry. It is capable of atomic layer deposition, in situ analysis of as-deposited samples via XPS and TPD, as well as deposition of size-selected metal clusters on substrates in ultra-high vacuum.

I have done plenty of data analysis at my work using different programming tools, however, ever since I laid my hands on Python, I just couldn't let it go. It made me fall in love with programming. It is so powerful and I even built my entire site with python django as my backend.

I will randomly post some stuff that I have learned about programming in my blog, mainly as a learning journal for myself and a way to motivate me to get better. My current focus, apart from chemistry of course, is machine learning and deep learning, as I can easily transfer my data analysis skill sets into these areas.

I am also a fan of homebrew, not a brewmaster though, but good enough to keep myself entertained.
